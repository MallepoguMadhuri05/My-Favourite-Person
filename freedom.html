Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi Jayanti is celebrated every year on 2nd October in honor of the birthday of the Father of the nation, Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, popularly known as Mahatma Gandhi who was born on this day in 1869. Fondly remembered as "Bapu", Mahatma Gandhi led the principle of non violence and also inspired movements for civil rights, freedom and non violence across the globe. Mahatma Gandhi dedicated his life to the aspect of discovering truth or Satya. Gandhiji also believed extensively in equality, brotherhood of man and non violence. Born in Gujarat, Mahatma Gandhi was one of the greatest freedom fighters the world has ever seen. It is due to the efforts of Mahatma Gandhi, "The father of the nation", along with other leaders, which led India to gain its independence and that Indians are living in a free country. Life of Mahatma Gandhi: Mahatma Gandhi was born Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi was born on 2nd October, 1869 in the coastal town of Porbandar in Gujarat. He was born in his ancestral home currently known as Kirti Mandir. Porbandar was at that time a part of the Bombay Presidency in British ruled India. Gandhiji’s father was Karamchand Gandhi and mother was Putlibai. His father, Karamchand Gandhi belonged to the Hindu Modh community served as a diwan of the Porbandar state in the Kathiawar Agency of British India. Gandhiji’s grandfather was Uttamchand Gandhi also called as Utta Gandhi. Gandhiji’s mother, Putlibai was from the Pranami Vaishnava community and was Karamchand’s fourth wife. Putli bai was a devout Jain and her Jain ideas and practices influenced Gandhi a lot. Gandhiji was also influenced by the Indian classics like the stories of Shravana and Harish Chandra.

Kalpana Chawla

Kalpana Chawla (March 17, 1962 – February 1, 2003) was an American astronaut and aerospace engineer, notable as the first woman of Indian origin to travel to space. Born in Karnal, Haryana, India, she developed an early passion for aviation and space, which she pursued despite societal and familial resistance. Education and Early Career Attended Tagore Baal Niketan Senior Secondary School in Karnal. Earned a Bachelor of Engineering in Aeronautical Engineering from Punjab Engineering College, Chandigarh, in 1982, becoming the first woman to receive this degree from the college. Moved to the United States in 1982 for higher studies: M.S. in Aerospace Engineering from the University of Texas at Arlington (1984). PhD in Aerospace Engineering from the University of Colorado Boulder (1988). Became a naturalized U.S. citizen in the early 1990s. NASA Career and Space Missions Joined NASA Ames Research Center in 1988, focusing on computational fluid dynamics and powered-lift aircraft research. Selected by NASA as an astronaut candidate in 1994, reporting to Johnson Space Center in 1995. First spaceflight: STS-87 aboard Space Shuttle Columbia in 1997, serving as mission specialist and robotic arm operator. She orbited Earth 252 times, traveling over 10.4 million miles and logging more than 372 hours in space. Second spaceflight: STS-107, also on Columbia, in 2003. The mission ended tragically when the shuttle disintegrated during reentry, killing all seven crew members, including Chawla.

A.P.J Abdul kalam

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam) was an eminent Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India, serving from 2002 to 2007. He was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, into a modest family—his father was a boat owner and imam at the local mosque. Despite financial hardships, Kalam excelled in academics, graduating in physics from St. Joseph’s College, Trichy, and later specializing in aeronautical engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology. Scientific Contributions: Kalam played a pivotal role in India’s space and defense programs. As Project Director at ISRO, he led the development of India’s first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), which successfully placed the Rohini satellite in orbit in 1980, making India a member of the exclusive Space Club. He was instrumental in developing the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and later, as Chief Executive of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP) at DRDO, he oversaw the creation of key missiles such as Agni and Prithvi, earning him the title “Missile Man of India”. Kalam played a crucial role in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, which established India as a nuclear weapons state. Presidency and Legacy: Elected as President with broad political support, Kalam was known as the “People’s President” for his accessibility and rapport with the public, especially youth. After his presidency, he dedicated himself to teaching, writing, and inspiring young minds, often emphasizing the importance of dreaming big and innovation. He received numerous honors, including the Bharat Ratna, India’s highest civilian award, and honorary doctorates from over 30 universities.

PV Sindhu

Pusarla Venkata Sindhu (PV Sindhu), born on 5 July 1995 in Hyderabad, is one of India’s most successful and celebrated badminton players. She is the first and only Indian to become a badminton world champion and the first Indian woman to win two Olympic medals: a silver at the 2016 Rio Olympics and a bronze at the 2020 Tokyo Olympics (held in 2021). Sindhu also became the first Indian woman to win a gold medal at the BWF World Championships in 2019. Career highlights: Broke into the BWF World Rankings top 20 at age 17 in 2012. Has won five medals at the BWF World Championships, making her only the second woman after China’s Zhang Ning to achieve this feat. Won her first Superseries title at the 2016 China Open and followed with multiple titles, including the 2018 BWF World Tour Finals. Commonwealth Games champion and three-time singles medallist at the event, along with a silver at the Asian Games and two Uber Cup bronze medals. Rose to a career-high world ranking of No. 2 in April 2017. Background and personal life: Sindhu is the daughter of former national-level volleyball players from Hyderabad, which contributed to her athletic upbringing. She began playing badminton at age eight, training under Mehboob Ali and later under Pullela Gopichand, a legendary Indian badminton coach. Known for her discipline and determination, she would travel 56 km daily for training as a young athlete. Alongside her sports career, she serves as a Deputy Collector in the Government of Andhra Pradesh. Awards and recognition: Recipient of India’s highest sports honors: the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna (now Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna) and the Arjuna Award. Civilian awards include the Padma Shri (2015) and Padma Bhushan (2020). Featured on Forbes’ list of highest-paid female athletes multiple times, reflecting her impact and popularity. Recent developments: As of 2025, Sindhu remains a leading figure in Indian badminton, inspiring the next generation and continuing to compete at the highest level.
